  Finding assessments

The conservation community who will use the recommendations from the assessments include conservation program managers from zoos, aquariums and other ex situ organizations, along with field researchers, amphibian conservationists and government wildlife departments. In addition, general visitors that are interested in the assessment process or in viewing the data for their own interests can also access completed assessments. All competed assessments can be viewed by all visitors to the site and assessment data is updated by the authors of each individual assessment.

Click on “View Assessments” at the top of any page, or on the Home page, click the “Find a species assessment” link to view completed assessments. You can perform a search by country, by species name, or by using a combination of both. To search for all species assessments within a country, use the drop-down menu to select the country (note: all countries for which assessments exist will be listed; although all species within a country may not have been assessed).

To search by species name, you can enter the scientific name (genus and/or species) or the common name. The search engine will find all species that contain all or part of the entry. Example: Enter “Acris”. Result: species with the genus Acris and Pseudacris.

The grid will show each species’ country, scientific name, common name, local common name and who performed the assessment. Clicking on the hyperlinked scientific name will display the assessment details.

Recent assessments listed on the Home Page can be accessed directly by clicking on the hyperlinked scientific name.
