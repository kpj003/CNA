  Viewing assessment results

Assessors may want to view their own assessments {link to Assessors’ guide - “Finding my assessments” help topic}, other assessments completed for their species that have a different distribution (and completed by a different assessor) or to check for other species assessments within the particular region/country where their species is found. You do not need to be logged in to view completed and approved assessments although you can also view draft assessments after logging in.

Assessment results for individual species can be viewed by selecting the species through the “View Assessments” link at the top of each page, through the “Find a species assessment” on the Home Page or by selecting a species under “Recent Assessments” on the Home Page. The Assessment Results include basic information for each species with recommended conservation actions, a map icon with a link to the IUCN Red List map, and answers to the questions.

Basic information and conservation action recommendations:

  • Scientific name
  • Date of assessment
  • Assessor name
  • Assessment status
  • Country or region
  • Order and Family
  • IUCN Global Red List category
  • IUCN National Red List category
  • Distribution
  • Evolutionary Distinctiveness score as generated by the Zoological Society of London EDGE (Evolutionarily Distinct & Globally Endangered) program. The score is assigned by AArk staff and cannot be edited by Assessors.
  • Conservation action recommendations

Clicking on the Red List map will take you to the distribution map included on the species’ IUCN Red List assessment page illustrating the species’ range. There are also links to the species’ Red List page and to images and species accounts on the AmphibiaWeb website.

Each question in the Assessment is identified by a short name and is represented using the question text with the Assessor’s response and comments. Clicking on the question number will take you to the definition of this question on the Questions and Answers help page. When an assessment is marked as complete and saved, the software will then generate the recommended conservation actions. A total score is calculated for each species assessment, based on the values of the answers provided by the Assessor. This score is then used to prioritize species within reports of recommended national and global conservation actions. The scores for each species are not displayed on the assessment results, as they are relevant only when compared with other species for prioritization purposes.

The “View Assessment” link will take you to a search page which contains only completed and approved assessments that have been done. The “Add Assessments” link will allow you to search for all assessments (including draft assessments) by other Assessors, and add an additional assessment, edit your own completed assessments, or complete your unfinished assessments.

On the search results grid, an Assessors’ own assessments are marked as either “Edit my assessment” or “Complete my assessment”, depending on the status of each assessment. However the only way to determine the status of other Assessors’ assessments is to view the actual assessment. In the species information at the top, there is an “Assessment Status” section which shows Complete or Incomplete.

When the “Show only my assessments” box is checked, the search results will include both incomplete and complete assessments done by the Assessor that is logged in.
